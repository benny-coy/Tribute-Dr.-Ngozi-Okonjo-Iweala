Dr. Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala


A model of an intelligent African woman
Currently, Dr. Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala is an economist and international development expert. The current director-general of World Trade Organisation, the first woman and black-woman to head WTO. She also sits on the Boards of Standard Chartered Bank, Twitter, Global Alliance for Vaccines and Immunization (GAVI), and the African Risk Capacity (ARC) among other organisations.

Here's is the timeline of Dr. Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala:


  • 1954 - Born in Ogwashi Ukwu, Nigeria.

  • 1973 - She arrived in the US as a teenager to study at Harvard University, graduating magna cum laude with an AB in Economics in 1976.

  • 1981 - she earned her Ph.D in regional economics and development from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) with a thesis titled Credit policy, rural financial markets, and Nigeria's agricultural development.

  • 1989-1991 - World bank, Special Assistant to the Senior Vice President, Operations.

  • 1995-1997 - Director of Institutional Change and Strategy.

  • 1997-2000 - Development economist at the World Bank as Vice President and Corporate Secretary. Working in the East Asia Region during the East Asian financial crisis.

  • 2000-2003 - Two duty tours in the Middle East Region, as Director, Operations (deputy vice-president) of the region.

  • 2003-2006 - First Female Minister of Finance of Federal Republic of Nigeria. In 2005, She led the Nigerian team that negotiated the cancellation of 60% of Nigeria’s external debt ($18 billion) with the Paris Club. The debt deal also included an innovative buy-back mechanism that wiped out Nigeria’s Paris Club debt and reduced the country’s external indebtedness from $35 billion to $5 billion.

  • 2006 - First female as Minister of Foreign Affairs of Nigeria, overseeing Nigeria’s External Relations.

  • 2007-2011 - She was Managing Director of The World Bank Group.

  • 2009 - She joined The Rockefeller Foundation board of trustees.

  • 2011-2015 - She was appointed a second term as the Minister of Finance and Economy of the Federal republic of Nigeria and head of Nigeria’s Presidential Economic team responsible for implementing a comprehensive home-grown economic reform program that stabilized the macro-economy and tripled the growth rate to an average 6% per annum over three years. Her reforms made particular progress in restoring macroeconomic stability, tripling growth, initiating a strong fight against corruption, and increasing transparency.

  • 2005 - she led the Nigerian team that negotiated the cancellation of 60% of Nigeria’s external debt ($18 billion) with the Paris Club. The debt deal also included an innovative buy-back mechanism that wiped out Nigeria’s Paris Club debt and reduced the country’s external indebtedness from $35 billion to $5 billion, among others.

She has also received numerous awards, among them are:


  • African of the year 2020, Forbes Africa.

  • 2017 Vanguard Award, Howard University

  • 2017 Madeleine K. Albright Global Development Award, Aspen Institute.

  • 2016 Global Fairness Award, Global Fairness Initiative.

  • Honorary Doctorate of Laws from Colby College in 2007.

  • Brown University in 2006, Honorary Doctorate of Humane Letters, Northern Caribbean University, Jamaica.

  • Euromoney Magazine Global Finance Minister of the year 2005.

  • Financial Times/The Banker African Finance Minister of the year 2005.

  • This Day Nigeria Minister of the Year 2004 and 2005.

  • Time Magazine European Hero 2004, etc.

"She is indeed my model of an intelligent African woman "--Patience Akpan.
